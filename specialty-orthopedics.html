Orthopedics & Oral Health

Bone health and oral health are inseparable. The jaw is bone. Osteoporosis affects it first. Rheumatoid arthritis and gum disease share identical inflammatory pathways — meaning the same disease process is destroying your joints and your gums simultaneously. And oral bacteria can infect artificial joints with devastating consequences.

🔬 The Connection

Rheumatoid Arthritis

RA and periodontitis share not just inflammatory pathways but the same specific trigger: citrullinated proteins produced by the gum bacteria P. gingivalis appear to initiate the autoimmune response that causes RA. Treating gum disease in RA patients measurably reduces joint disease activity scores.

Artificial Joint Infections

Oral bacteria — particularly Streptococcus — can seed artificial hip and knee joints via the bloodstream, causing devastating infections that require joint removal. Dental clearance before joint replacement surgery and antibiotic prophylaxis before dental procedures afterward is critical.

Osteoporosis & Jaw Bone Loss

Bone density loss from osteoporosis affects the jaw as much as the spine and hip. Tooth loss is an early and visible sign of systemic bone loss. Bisphosphonate medications prescribed for osteoporosis carry a serious risk of osteonecrosis of the jaw — dentists must know before any extraction.

✅ What Orthopedic Surgeons Should Know
🦷Require dental clearance and treat all active oral infections before any joint replacement surgery
💊Educate patients on bisphosphonate-related osteonecrosis of the jaw — dentists must know before extractions
🔗Refer RA patients to periodontists — treating gum disease improves joint disease outcomes
📋Consider jaw bone density as a proxy for systemic osteoporosis in relevant patients
✅ What Patients Can Do
💬Tell your orthopedic surgeon about any upcoming dental work — timing and antibiotics matter
🦷Get a full dental checkup before any joint replacement surgery
📅After a joint replacement: tell your dentist — they may need to prescribe antibiotics before dental procedures for life
💊If on bisphosphonates: never get a tooth extracted without discussing with your prescribing doctor first
Questions to Ask Your Orthopedic Surgeon
"Do I need dental clearance before my joint replacement surgery?"
"Will I need antibiotic prophylaxis before dental procedures after my joint replacement?"
"My bisphosphonate medication — what does it mean for my dental care?"
"Could my gum disease be worsening my rheumatoid arthritis?"
