The Problem

Most people are never told this

Dentistry and medicine have operated in separate silos for over a century. Patients with heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and pregnancy complications are almost never told their oral health is connected. We're changing that.

🦷

Gum disease affects your whole body

Oral bacteria enter your bloodstream through inflamed gums and have been found in arterial plaques, brain tissue, cancer tumors, and artificial joints.

🔗

Dentistry and medicine don't talk

Cardiologists never ask about gum health. OBGYNs skip dental care. ICU teams miss oral care protocols that could save lives. The silos cost patients.

🌍

Underserved communities suffer most

Veterans, low-income families, pregnant women, and people with chronic illness have the highest oral health needs and the least access to guidance.

💡

The information exists — but isn't accessible

The CDC, NIH, and ADA have the research. But it's written for clinicians, not patients. SmileMom translates it into plain language for everyone.

How SmileMom Works

Three layers of support

Not a blog. Not a brochure. A guided experience that meets you where you are — whether you're pregnant, managing a chronic disease, or serving in the military.

01

Find your community

Pregnant women, athletes, cancer patients, veterans, diabetics — every community has its own dedicated guide built around their specific oral health needs and risks.

02

Get personalized guidance

Take the quiz and receive a tailored plan — what's happening in your mouth, what to do today, what to watch for, and when to seek care.

03

Track your progress

Use the dashboard to check off daily habits, log symptoms, build streaks, and monitor your oral health throughout your journey.
