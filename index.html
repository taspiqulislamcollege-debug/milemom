The Problem

Most women are never told this

Prenatal care covers a lot — but oral health is almost always missing from the conversation.

🦷

Pregnancy changes your teeth and gums

Hormonal shifts increase blood flow to gum tissue, making them more sensitive and reactive to bacteria — even with good hygiene.

🤢

Morning sickness increases enamel risk

Frequent vomiting exposes teeth to stomach acid. Without a rinsing routine, enamel can erode permanently.

🩸

Bleeding gums need monitoring

Up to 75% of pregnant women experience pregnancy gingivitis — common, but not something to ignore without a plan.

🔗

Dental and prenatal care feel disconnected

OBGYNs and dentists rarely coordinate. Most women are left to guess what's normal on their own.

Why It Matters

Oral health affects more than your smile

Research consistently links untreated gum disease during pregnancy to serious outcomes. Yet most women are never told to pay attention to their oral health.

75%

Experience pregnancy gingivitis

Most pregnant women develop gum inflammation — yet the majority are never given guidance on how to manage it. Source: CDC

3×

Higher cavity risk for baby

Children of mothers with untreated cavities during pregnancy are over 3x more likely to develop cavities. Source: CDC

56%

Skip the dentist while pregnant

More than half of pregnant women do not visit a dentist during pregnancy — often not knowing it's safe. Source: CDC
