The Science

How your mouth affects
the rest of your body

Three proven biological pathways explain why what happens in your mouth doesn't stay in your mouth.

01

Bacterial Translocation

Inflamed gum tissue acts like an open wound — allowing oral bacteria to enter the bloodstream directly. These bacteria have been detected in arterial plaques, brain tissue, joint fluid, and cancer tumors in other parts of the body. The mouth is literally seeding infection elsewhere.

02

Systemic Inflammation

Gum disease triggers the immune system to release pro-inflammatory compounds — interleukin-1, TNF-alpha, and others — that circulate throughout the body. This chronic low-grade inflammation worsens heart disease, makes insulin resistance worse, accelerates Alzheimer's progression, and degrades joint tissue.

03

Shared Risk Factors

Smoking, stress, poor diet, and socioeconomic hardship drive both oral and systemic disease simultaneously. This means treating one condition creates an opportunity to intervene on others. Dentists who screen for diabetes, and physicians who ask about gum health, catch disease earlier for both.
